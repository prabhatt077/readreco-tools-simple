How exact age is calculated

This calculator finds your exact age using calendar-based arithmetic — subtracting your birth date from today's date year by year, month by month, and day by day — rather than simply dividing total days lived by 365. This matters because months have different lengths and leap years add an extra day, so a naive day-count calculation can be off by several days.

Why your next birthday countdown matters

The countdown shows exactly how many days remain until your next birthday, accounting for whether it has already passed this year and rolling over to next year if so.

Frequently asked questions

Why not just divide my age in days by 365?

Because months vary in length and leap years add extra days, dividing by 365 can miscalculate your age by several days. Calendar-based subtraction is accurate.

What happens if I was born on February 29?

In non-leap years, your birthday countdown rolls to the nearest equivalent date, since February 29 doesn't exist every year.
